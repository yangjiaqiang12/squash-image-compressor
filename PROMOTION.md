# Squash Promotion Plan

## 1. Dev.to Post (Primary)

Title: **I Built a Free Image Compressor That Never Uploads Your Files — Here's Why That Matters**

```markdown
## The Problem

Every time you use an online image compressor, your files get uploaded to someone's server. Tinypng.com? Uploads. Compressor.io? Uploads. Even the "free" ones collect your data.

I've always been uncomfortable with this. Images can contain sensitive information — screenshots of conversations, private photos, business documents. Why should compressing them require handing them to a stranger?

## The Solution: Squash

[Squash](https://yangjiaqiang12.github.io/squash-image-compressor/) is a free, open-source image compressor that runs **entirely in your browser**.

- 🚫 No uploads — everything stays on your device
- ⚡ Instant compression — no waiting for network round-trips
- 🎨 Multi-format support — JPEG, PNG, WebP
- 📦 Batch processing — compress multiple images at once
- 🌓 Dark mode — works great at night
- 💰 Completely free — no limits, no watermarks

## How It Works

Squash uses the browser's Canvas API to decode images, apply compression settings, and re-encode them at your chosen quality level. The heavy lifting happens on your device's hardware — not on some server farm.

## Why I Built This

I wanted a tool that respects privacy. Not as a marketing slogan — as a technical guarantee. Your images literally cannot be uploaded because there's no server to upload them to. It's all client-side.
```
